The Famous Tornado Module

History

The tornado module I have now is actually the second instance of such a module.

Credit for both tornado modules I have belongs to Gerry LaPlace, a member of the Radcliff Model Railroad Association of Radcliff, KY.

He was inspired to do a module after he and I saw the N Trak version at the NMRA National Train Show in Cincinnati, Ohio, back in 2005. This one was created by an N Trak member from St. Louis, Missouri.

When I introduced N Scale T-Trak to the NMRA back in 2007, Gerry saw an opportunity to try to make a similar module, but obviously smaller. He could not get the tornado to move up and down like on the N Trak module, but he did find an electronic module that simulated the thunder sound and came with a flashing light to indicate lightning strikes.

Since I was doing more shows than Gerry, I purchased it and started touring around with it at shows, and it became a huge hit,

In 2018, for almost 11 years, the tornado module could not be maintained. So, I commissioned Gerry to make another one. Hence, Tornado Module 2.0 was created and debuted at Trainfest in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, and is alive and well today.
